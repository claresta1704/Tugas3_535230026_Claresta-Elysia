Drawing has been a part of my life since I was a child. I've gone through the process of learning to draw from scratch. I may not be a professional like other well-known artists, but I want to share with you the tips I’ve learned throughout my self-taught journey in drawing.
  • Start by redrawing references
    I'm not teaching you to tracing, okay? Tracing is like placing the reference underneath your canvas and copying everything on your reference, that's not art. What I mean is to redraw by looking. Try to place your reference close to your canvas and quickly shift your gaze between your canvas and the reference. Try to make an exact replica to train your skills. This method is very effective to train your skills!
  • Practice with sketches
    Sketching is very important. It's the foundation of everything. You can start by learning how to build the framework of a face first. I recommend watching the YouTube channel ‘Mmmmonexx’ Once you become proficient at shading and linework, you'll become more comfortable with sketching.
  • Study light and shadow
    By understanding the direction of light and shadows, your drawings will look more lifelike. You can study this from everyday objects around you. For example, how does light from above hit a ball? Observe its shadow, and you can practice drawing it right there on the spot.
  • Learn color theory
    I admit, color theory can be challenging to grasp. You don’t need to start with the more complicated aspects of it. Begin with the basics, like color combinations. Study complementary colors and analogous colors. Understanding this will help your artwork have a more harmonious and beautiful color scheme.
I started crocheting about a year ago. Crocheting takes quite a long time, but it keeps you curious about the result, so you can’t stop making it. For crocheting, you need two basic materials: a hook and yarn. Without a hook, you can't crochet. You can plan what you want to make, so you can adjust the color of the yarn you need. Crochet hooks and yarn come in different sizes, depending on how big you want your creation to be. There are also different types of yarn to consider, especially when crocheting clothing. Some yarns, like acrylic, are not suitable for making clothes. I don’t recommend making clothes for beginner crocheters, as it's quite difficult and takes a very long time! I suggest starting with small dolls first. You can easily find tutorials on YouTube for crocheting whatever you want. Soon, you'll become familiar with the following abbreviations:
  • sc (single crochet)
  • dc (double crochet)
  • hdc (half double crochet)
  • tc (triple crochet)
  • inc (increase)
  • dec (decrease)
  • fo (fasten off)
I started creating art with clay about a year ago. Making clay art isn’t as difficult as you might think. First, it’s important to decide what kind of art you want to create. If you want to make a display or a sculpture, it’s better to use air-dry clay because of its solid and hard texture. However, if you want to make something simpler, you can use polymer clay. Using air-dry clay might be a bit challenging because its texture is harder compared to polymer clay.
Of course, you’ll need the clay first. To make your work easier, I recommend purchasing clay sculpting tools, which will help you shape and carve the clay into your desired form.
Since it’s called "air-dry clay," this clay will harden when exposed to air, so if you want to store your leftover air-dry clay, keep it in an airtight container to prevent it from drying out completely. Once you’ve finished your project, don’t dry it directly in front of a fan. Drying it too quickly will make the outer part of your project hard, while the inside remains wet, which can cause cracks. Let the air naturally dry your project at its own pace.
For coloring, you can mix dye directly into the clay before shaping if you’re using polymer clay, as its texture is softer and easy to mix with anything. As for air-dry clay, you can paint your project with acrylic paint once it’s dry. And I have an extra tip for air-dry clay: it's better to sand your work for a smoother and more polished result before adding color. But don’t sand polymer clay because it will remain smooth even after it dries.
